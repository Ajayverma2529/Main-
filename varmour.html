Defending the enterprise infrastructure is becoming harder in the age of digital business.

The interconnected nature of the applications, devices, users and data - the relationships and dependencies - that drive a digital enterprise also creates a myriad of exploitation paths internally and externally, leading to a bigger and more vulnerable cyber attack surface.

State of IT: Hyperconnected

Hundreds of thousands of interconnected apps, devices, users and data drive a digital enterprise across different geographies and constituents.

Pain point: Larger attack surface

Attack surface grows exponentially with each additional asset or user to support business strategy.

State of IT: Increasingly Distributed

More and more applications are running on multiple platforms, including public cloud, SaaS, and datacenters.

Pain point: More visibility gaps

Inability to see or track applications and their relationships results in security vulnerabilities and incomplete cyber hygiene that cannot be solved by existing solutions.

State of IT: Moving to cloud

There are far more cloud workloads and applications compared to the last five years. Companies with most or all IT in the cloud will increase from 41% to 63% in the next 18 months.

(Cloud Computing Study 2022, Foundry)

Pain point: Blind to new security risks

Inability to validate or quantify enterprise-wide systemic risks as modern attack vectors leverage interconnected infrastructure that is unknown and unsecured.

State of IT: Public access

Digital assets are increasingly accessed by customers and suppliers and have additional compliance requirements to fulfill. In 2H 2020, public-facing exposed databases increased 16% to 166,000.

(“308,000 exposed databases discovered, proper management is key” Help Net Security, April 29, 2022)

Pain point: Compliance burden

Lack of automated auditing/reporting on compliance to fulfill recurring regulatory requirements for SOC2, PCI, HIPAA, etc. in public-facing applications.

Internet of Things (IoT)

The Internet of Things (IoT) shows increasing potential to connect billions of services and devices anytime, anywhere. Applications include smart homes and buildings, connected cars, wearables and smart fabrics, healthcare, smart cities, and public transportation, as well as applications that may drive the next industrial revolution.

Enabling technologies for IoT include cellular, satellite, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, NFC, RFID, ZigBee, LPWAN, and Ethernet.

Industrial & Electrical

Electrical and Industrial test equipment is used to maintain a variety of electrical apparatus and electrical distribution systems. These tools perform tests on transformers, switches, circuit breakers, protective relays, rotating machinery, and associated cabling. Oftentimes, a fault is simulated and the performance of the device or system under test is monitored for compliance. With infrared imagers (thermography), entire electrical systems can be inspected for trouble spots.
